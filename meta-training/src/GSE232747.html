GSE232747

  • Series_title: Transcriptomics Analysis of Diploid and Tetraploid Worms Exposed to DNA-Damaging Chemotherapeutics
  • Series_summary: To establish the consequences of whole-animal tetraploidy, we generated stable C. elegans worm strains that are tetraploid in every cell in their body and exposed them to DNA damaging chemotherapeutics doxorubicin and cisplatin at doses that do not cause growth delay. Exposures were carried out for 48 h beginning at L1 stage.
  • Series_overall_design: Samples include diploid and tetraploid ploidy groups, each exposed to vehicle (CTRL), 5 micromolar cisplatin (CIS), or 5 micromolar doxorubicin (DOX), in three biological replicates (1,2, and 3)
  • Series_type: Expression profiling by high throughput sequencing
